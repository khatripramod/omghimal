Everest Base Camp Trek

Journey to the foot of the world's highest peak through breathtaking Himalayas.

Overview

The Everest Base Camp (EBC) trek is one of the most iconic and sought-after trekking routes in the world, attracting adventurers from all corners of the globe. Stretching through the breathtaking Khumbu Valley in Nepal, this incredible journey takes trekkers on an unforgettable adventure, offering panoramic views of the worldâ€™s highest peak, Mount Everest, alongside a host of other majestic Himalayan mountains, including Lhotse, Nuptse, and Makalu.

The trek combines the thrill of high-altitude adventure with rich cultural experiences, as trekkers pass through Sherpa villages, ancient monasteries, and traditional Buddhist stupas. Along the way, visitors get a chance to interact with the local Sherpa people, learning about their unique customs, traditions, and lifestyles. These encounters offer a deep dive into the cultural heritage of the region, making the trek as much about the people as it is about the natural surroundings.

The landscapes encountered on the EBC trek are nothing short of spectacular. From lush green forests to rugged alpine terrain, icy rivers, and rocky moraines, every day brings a new scene of natural beauty. Trekkers also pass through Sagarmatha National Park, a UNESCO World Heritage Site, home to diverse wildlife such as the Himalayan tahr, snow leopards, and various bird species. The route is dotted with prayer flags and stone mani walls, which add to the spiritual and serene atmosphere of the trek.

Ultimately, the journey to Everest Base Camp is not just about reaching a destination; it is about embracing the challenges of the trek, taking in the awe-inspiring views, and experiencing the warmth and hospitality of the Sherpa people. The sense of achievement when finally standing at the base of Everest, looking up at its towering presence, is an experience that stays with trekkers for a lifetime.

Trek Itinerary

Day 1
Flight from Kathmandu to Lukla, trek to Phakding (2,610m)

Begin your adventure with a scenic flight to Lukla, followed by an easy trek to Phakding.

Day 2
Trek to Namche Bazaar (3,440m)

Challenging ascent to the vibrant Sherpa capital of Namche Bazaar.

Day 3
Acclimatization Day in Namche Bazaar

Rest and acclimatize. Explore Namche Bazaar and enjoy panoramic views of Everest and other peaks.

Day 4
Trek to Tengboche (3,860m)

Descend to the Dudh Koshi River, cross a bridge, and make your way up to the beautiful village of Tengboche, home to a famous monastery.

Day 5
Trek to Dingboche (4,410m)

Head further up the valley, passing through Pangboche and reaching the peaceful village of Dingboche.

Day 6
Acclimatization Day in Dingboche

Take a day to acclimatize, with an optional hike up to Nagarjun Hill for stunning views of Everest, Lhotse, and Makalu.

Day 7
Trek to Lobuche (4,910m)

Continue the trek to Lobuche, passing through the memorials of fallen climbers.

Day 8
Trek to Everest Base Camp (5,364m), Return to Gorak Shep (5,140m)

Reach Everest Base Camp for a truly unforgettable experience, then return to Gorak Shep for the night.

Day 9
Hike to Kala Patthar (5,545m), Trek down to Pheriche (4,240m)

Early morning hike to Kala Patthar for sunrise views of Everest, then descend to Pheriche for rest.

Day 10
Trek back to Namche Bazaar

Retrace your steps back to Namche Bazaar, enjoying the downhill trek and beautiful scenery.

Day 11
Trek to Lukla

Complete the trek by descending to Lukla, where youâ€™ll take a flight back to Kathmandu.
